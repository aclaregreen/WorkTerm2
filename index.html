Introduction

Welcome to my second Co-op work term report! My name is Andrew Clare-Green, and I am a 3rd-year Computer Science student at the University of Guelph. This report highlights my experiences and achievements during my second work term as a Scheduling Systems Support Co-op student at the University of Guelph. I will discuss my accomplishments, the skills I developed, and reflect on my learning goals.

About the Employer

The University of Guelph, located in Guelph, Ontario, Canada, is home to nearly 30,000 students, offering 94 undergraduate degrees, 48 graduate programs, and 6 associate degrees. The university was established on May 8th, 1964, following the merger of the Ontario Agricultural College (OAC), the Ontario Veterinary College, and the Macdonald Institute. Later that year, Wellington College was added, expanding the university's opportunities in arts and sciences

During this term, I worked in the Scheduling Systems department, part of the Office of Registrarial Services. The department oversees course scheduling, exam timetables, and classroom allocations, ensuring smooth academic operations for the university.

Goals

At the start of my work term, I set three goals:

  • Improve technical skills: Deepen my expertise in database management and scripting by working extensively with SQL and system scheduling tools.
  • Enhance organizational efficiency: Optimize internal processes for maintaining course schedules by automating repetitive tasks using Python scripts.
  • Collaborate effectively: Work closely with cross-functional teams to resolve scheduling conflicts and provide technical support.

Job Description

As a Scheduling Systems Support Co-op student, my responsibilities included:

  • Maintaining and updating the Infosilem course scheduling database.
  • Assisting in resolving scheduling conflicts for courses and exams.
  • Automating repetitive tasks using Python, improving process efficiency.
  • Debugging and optimizing SQL queries to ensure the accuracy of scheduling data.
  • Providing technical support to faculty and staff on scheduling tools and procedures.

Conclusions

This position gave me practical experience in database management, problem-solving, and automation. It enhanced my technical, analytical, and interpersonal skills, preparing me for future challenges in the tech industry.

Acknowledgments

I sincerely thank my supervisor, Tammy Arsenault-Irving, for her guidance and support throughout my work term. Tammy assisted me whenever we encountered problems my coworker and I got stuck on.
